Hunza

Where the Karakoram meets the sky — the valley of longevity, turquoise lakes, and ancient kingdoms.

🌟 Overview

The Eighth Wonder

Nestled in the embrace of three mighty mountain ranges — the Karakoram, Himalaya, and Hindu Kush — Hunza Valley is a place so stunning that it has been called the inspiration for Shangri-La, the mythical Himalayan paradise.

The people of Hunza are renowned for their extraordinary longevity and health, a diet of apricots and walnuts, and a culture of warm hospitality that has welcomed silk road travelers for millennia. Today, it remains one of Earth's most spectacular destinations.

🗺️ Must-Visit

Top Attractions

Natural wonders and ancient history collide in this high-altitude paradise.

🌊

Attabad Lake

Born from a 2010 landslide, this surreal turquoise lake stretches 21 km through the Karakoram, its vivid blue waters framed by dramatic rocky cliffs. Boat tours are a must.

🏯

Baltit Fort

This 700-year-old fortress overlooks Karimabad from a rocky perch, offering panoramic views of the valley and Ultar Sar peak. A UNESCO candidate site and Hunza's crown jewel.

🌸

Cherry Blossom Fields

Each April, Hunza explodes in pink and white as apricot and cherry trees blossom simultaneously — one of the world's most beautiful natural spectacles.

🛣️

Karakoram Highway

The 8th Wonder of the World — a 1,300km engineering marvel connecting Pakistan to China through the mightiest mountains on the planet. The drive itself is the destination.

🏰

Altit Fort

Said to be over 900 years old, Altit is the oldest fort in Hunza. Its wooden architecture and riverside location make it a photographer's dream.

🏔️

Rakaposhi View Point

From Danyore, you can see Rakaposhi — one of the world's most breathtaking peaks — in its full 7,788m glory, rising directly from the valley floor in a single dramatic sweep.

📅 Itinerary

5-Day Hunza Journey

The classic route through the valley of longevity.

Day 1
Arrive in Gilgit → Karimabad
Fly to Gilgit, then a scenic 2-hour drive to Karimabad. Stop at Rakaposhi viewpoint for stunning photos. Settle in and enjoy sunset from Baltit Fort viewpoint.
Day 2
Baltit Fort & Karimabad Bazaar
Explore the historic Baltit Fort, then wander through Karimabad's charming market for dried apricots, gemstones, and handicrafts.
Day 3
Attabad Lake Boat Tour
Drive to the otherworldly Attabad Lake. Take a boat tour through the vivid turquoise water, surrounded by vertical cliff walls. Picnic by the lakeside.
Day 4
Passu Cones & Hussaini Bridge
Trek to see the dramatic Passu Cathedral peaks, then cross the legendary Hussaini suspension bridge — one of the most thrilling bridge crossings in the world.
Day 5
Altit Fort & Eagle's Nest
Morning at Altit Fort, then drive up to Eagle's Nest for a panoramic 360° view of the entire Hunza Valley with Rakaposhi and Ultar Sar dominating the skyline.
Departure
Return to Gilgit
Final morning stroll through apricot orchards, then drive back to Gilgit for your flight. Pack your memories — and some dried apricots!

💡 Traveller Tips

Before You Go

🎫Book Gilgit-Baltistan permits early — required for foreign nationals and recommended for all visitors.
⛰️Acclimatize properly — the altitude can cause headaches. Rest your first day and stay hydrated.
📶Mobile signal is limited. Download offline maps (Maps.me) and inform someone of your itinerary.
🌸For cherry blossoms, arrive between late March and mid-April — exact timing varies each year.
🥾Pack proper trekking shoes, layers for cold mornings/evenings, and a good rain jacket.
