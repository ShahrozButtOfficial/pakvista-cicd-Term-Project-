Lahore

Where Mughal grandeur meets modern vibrancy — Lahore is Pakistan's beating cultural heart.

🌟 Overview

City of Gardens & Glory

Lahore — the "Paris of Asia" — has been a center of civilization for over a thousand years. As the capital of the Mughal Empire at its zenith, it was graced with some of the most breathtaking architecture the world has ever seen.

Today, it's a city that pulses with life: its bazaars overflow with color and fragrance, its food streets sizzle through the night, its universities buzz with students, and its heritage sites stand as proud monuments to past glory.

🗺️ Must-Visit

Top Attractions

Lahore's iconic landmarks — each a chapter in a living history book.

🕌

Badshahi Mosque

Built in 1673 by Emperor Aurangzeb, this is one of the largest mosques in the world. Its red sandstone exterior and white marble domes are an architectural masterpiece.

🏰

Lahore Fort (Shahi Qila)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this fortress dates to the 11th century and contains palaces, gardens and the stunning Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors).

🌿

Shalimar Gardens

Commissioned by Shah Jahan in 1641, these UNESCO-listed terraced Mughal gardens span 40 hectares of fountains, pavilions, and blossom trees.

🛍️

Anarkali Bazaar

One of Asia's oldest markets, alive since the 16th century with fabrics, jewelry, spices, street food, and the pulse of old Lahore.

🎨

Wazir Khan Mosque

Built in 1635, this gem of Mughal architecture is famous for its exquisite faience tile work — a kaleidoscope of color inside the Walled City.

🍖

Food Street Gawalmandi

Legendary among foodies worldwide — Nihari, Paye, Biryani and Lahori Fried Fish served under fairy lights with a view of the lit Badshahi Mosque.

📅 Itinerary

3-Day Lahore Plan

The perfect route to soak in the best of Lahore.

Day 1 — Morning
The Walled City
Start at Delhi Gate, walk through Wazir Khan Mosque, explore the Walled City's narrow alleys and spice shops.
Day 1 — Afternoon
Lahore Fort & Badshahi Mosque
Explore Shahi Qila's palaces and Sheesh Mahal, then the awe-inspiring Badshahi Mosque as the sun dips low.
Day 1 — Evening
Food Street Gawalmandi
Feast on Lahori delicacies under the glittering lights of Food Street with the mosque illuminated in the background.
Day 2
Shalimar Gardens & Anarkali
Spend the morning at the serene Mughal gardens, then afternoon at Anarkali Bazaar for shopping and chai.
Day 3 — Morning
Lahore Museum & Mall Road
Visit one of South Asia's finest museums, then stroll the colonial-era Mall Road lined with Victorian architecture.
Day 3 — Evening
Liberty Market & Wagah Border
Shop at Liberty Market then head to Wagah Border at sunset for the dramatic flag-lowering ceremony.

💡 Traveller Tips

Before You Go

👗Dress modestly when visiting mosques and historical sites — scarves are recommended for women.
🚗Use Careem or inDrive for safe, affordable rides. Negotiate rickshaw prices before boarding.
🍽️Try the local Lahori breakfast — Halwa Puri, Nihari, and Channay with hot chai.
📸Golden hour at Badshahi Mosque (just after Asr prayer) gives the most stunning photographs.
💰Bargain at bazaars — vendors expect it! Starting at 50% of the asking price is reasonable.
